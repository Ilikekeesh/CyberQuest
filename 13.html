Cryptography refers to the field of hiding messages. While cryptography as a concept is almost 4000 years old, it's a constantly developing field, even now. Modern improvements in cryptography include quantum cryptography, a theoretically unbreakable encryption.

In order to understand cybersecurity, a basic understanding of hiding messages is necessary. Today, nearly everything stored online is encrypted so that hackers intercepting data can't read it without the key. "Keys" in cryptography act as a secret piece of information shared between the sender and recipient. For example, if Bob is sending Alice a message, they can meet in person and decide on a key to use, usually a word, number, or whole phrase depending on the style of encryption. Then, when Bob sends the message, no one else can read it other than Alice, since only she has the key.
